CLIMATE CHANGE

& How it's Affecting Our Food

Our food supply is much more affected by climate change than you probably thought. Even though global warming may seem to be slight at a 1-2° Fahrenheit change since 1880, the effect is disproportionate, and a tiny change can still snowball into catastrophic changes in our environment. These kinds of changes can be enough to disrupt the production of food. Grains are hit by extreme heat in countries that they’re grown in. Soybeans and rice also are threatened. Other weather patterns can be dangerous too, such as unusually heavy rains causing locust outbreaks in Northeastern Africa, eating many of the crops that would be eaten by humans. Wildfires caused by excessive heat and hurricanes and storms caused by warming oceans will also cause crop destruction.

If that wasn’t bad enough, the decline in food production isn’t even — in fact the nations hit the hardest will be poorer, third-world countries. This could also create even more conflict, because the amount of fertile land on Earth will dwindle. The increased scarcity of food will lead to increased prices, making it harder for low-income people to afford food. Extreme food shortages and other weather disasters will cause many people of poorer countries to migrate, searching for food security, complicating immigration politics. Because most countries trade for food from other countries, climate change poses an enormous threat to the food security of people all around the world.
